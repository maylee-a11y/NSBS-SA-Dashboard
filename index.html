Winning post playbook why the breakout worked — and how to repeat it

Carousel Collab 18 Jun · with @arrazinmahmood + @razinarchitects
“Timeless design isn't about appearance” — Masjid Daing Abdul Rahman

What made it win — in order of impact

1
The collab unlocked someone else's audience. A collab post publishes to both accounts' feeds at once — SAA has 59 followers, @razinarchitects has an established architect base. That's why 95.5% of reach was non-followers. Tagging a named person (@arrazinmahmood), not just the firm, added a second personal surface.
2
Feed-native carousel compounded the reach. 71% of views came from Feed (not the crowded Reels tab). 3,227 views on 1,388 reach = ~2.3 views/person — people swiped through cards, and Instagram reads swipe-through as quality and extends distribution. Engagement literally bought more reach.
3
One striking, real hero project did the thumb-stopping. The cover card alone earned 76 of the 140 likes (54%) — a recognizable mosque with a leaf-pattern mashrabiya and a named 2024 winner. A concrete, culturally specific project beats generic "submit your project" promo.
4
High "pass-it-on" signals. 13 shares + 8 saves + 6 reposts = 27 forward/keep actions. Shares are the highest-value signal Instagram weights (they push the post into DMs & stories) — exactly the reference-style content carousels are built for. 11.9% engagement rate on reach.
5
An opinionated, quote-led caption. A debatable one-line thesis ("isn't about appearance — it's about being truthful to its time, place, and people") invites agreement and tagging, closed with an explicit "Follow us" CTA.

Do more of

  • Co-create with the featured architect/firm and tag the person, not just the brand handle
  • Lead carousels with one arresting real project on the cover card
  • Anchor on a named winner + a debatable one-line thesis
  • Make it save- and share-worthy — reference-style "keep this" content
The repeatable formula: collab carousel × one striking real project on the cover × a named architect partner × an opinionated one-line thesis. It isn't simply "do collabs" — the 15 Jun reel was also a @razinarchitects collab and reached only 509. The win was the collaborator's audience + a feed-native carousel + a thumb-stopping hero image, not the collab alone.
